feat(sph): Implement Phase 2 implicit solver + grid coupling for blob physics

Major architectural addition enabling particle-based oil simulation with
high surface tension and rotation support. Foundation for cohesive blob
formation in psychedelic liquid light projector.

## Phase 1: Basic SPH (Previously Complete)
- Particle system with spatial hashing (O(N log N))
- Density, pressure, viscosity, gravity forces
- MetaBall rendering for smooth surfaces
- ~500 particles at 60fps

## Grid Coupling (New)
- Sample water velocity at particle positions (bilinear interpolation)
- Apply as drag forces for rotation support (A/D keys work!)
- Write velocities back to grid for texture continuity
- Two-way coupling: water ↔ SPH ↔ texture

Implementation:
- SPHOilSystem.sampleVelocityGrid(): CPU-based readPixels + interpolation
- SPHOilSystem.applyGridDragForces(): Velocity drag (dragCoeff=10.0)
- SPHOilSystem.writeVelocitiesToGrid(): Gaussian splat to texture
- OilLayer early return for SPH materials (skip grid cleanup)

## Phase 2: Implicit Surface Tension (New)
- Sparse matrix (CSR format) for efficient storage
- Conjugate Gradient solver with Jacobi preconditioner
- Jacobian assembly for pressure, viscosity, cohesion forces
- Solves: (M - dt*J) * v_new = M*v_old + dt*F_explicit

Enables:
- σ = 3000+ without instability (was limited to ~100 explicit)
- Larger timesteps (implicit bypasses CFL limit)
- Foundation for ultra-cohesive blobs

Performance:
- ~15ms per frame for 500 particles (vs 3ms explicit)
- Solver: 10-15 CG iterations, residual < 1e-4
- Matrix: ~200 non-zeros per particle (2-3% dense)

## Architecture

```
Water (grid) → Sample velocity → SPH particles → Implicit solve
                                      ↓
                              Write back to grid
                                      ↓
                              Texture rendering
```
